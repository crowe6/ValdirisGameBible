Chapter III
Magic System

Five elemental magic types, each tied to a race affinity and a tower. Dark magic is the rarest — available to all races but with unique costs.

Fire Magic
Applies Burn (4% HP/s, stacks 3x). Empowered in Tower of Emberveil. Full access for Humans, limited for BeastFolk (rage-state only). Countered by Water magic and Rain weather.
Water Magic
Applies Chill (−35% speed). Chill + Earth = Freeze (1.5s hard stun). Full access for Elves. Humans can unlock via secret questline. Empowered in Tower of Tidehollow.
Earth Magic
Applies Root (2–4s immobilize). Full access for Elves and BeastFolk. Can reshape terrain in high tiers. Empowered in Tower of Stonecrown. Counters gravity anomalies.
Light Magic
Applies Blind (no crit, −detection). Full access for Humans. Can parry magic projectiles. Empowered in Tower of Solaran (+40%). Dark magic weakened 50% in Light zones.
Dark Magic
Applies Curse (−20% magic resist). Apex-only unlock for most races. Corrupts Elf Aura stat (Voidtouched branch). Natural for BeastFolk. Empowered in Tower of Voidspire (+40%).
Storm (Tower Element)
Not a castable magic type — adds +15% lightning damage to all spells in Tower of Stormreach. BeastFolk Berserker absorbs lightning strikes for Strength stacks. Crosses all race affinities.

Chapter V
The Elemental Gods

Six ancient gods, each embodying two elements. Their dual nature defines their fighting style, weaknesses, and the divine power they grant upon defeat.

IGN
Ignareth the Undying Flame
God of Fire & Storm — Tower of Emberveil
FireStorm
Born from the first lightning strike that ever ignited the world, Ignareth is both the spark and the inferno. He embodies uncontrolled destruction — Fire as raw heat, Storm as the chaos that fans the flame. He is not malicious; he simply burns. Ancient texts describe him as a towering figure of molten iron wreathed in perpetual lightning, whose footsteps leave scorched craters and whose voice is the sound of thunder inside a volcano.
Elements
Fire (primary) + Storm (secondary). All attacks deal burn and chain lightning simultaneously.
Weakness
Water magic (extinguishes) and Earth magic (grounds the lightning). Elf Tidecaller and Earthwarden excel here.
Fight mechanic
Phase 3: summons a permanent storm — lightning strikes every 8s. Players must redirect storm energy with the floor's lightning rods while dealing damage.
Divine power granted
Ignareth's Wrath — rains meteors across 30m radius for 8s. Stat boost: Strength +25%. Visual: ember trails on movement. World power: ignites the Ashen Wastes.
VAE
Vaelmur the Endless Deep
God of Water & Earth — Tower of Tidehollow
WaterEarth
Vaelmur is patience given divine form. He is the tide that carves stone over millennia — Water as time and persistence, Earth as the immovable foundation beneath all things. He is the oldest of the gods, having existed since the first ocean touched the first shore. His form shifts between a massive aquatic creature and a stone colossus, the two halves of his nature cycling as the fight progresses. He does not hate challengers. He tests them.
Elements
Water (primary) + Earth (secondary). Tidal waves and stone golems simultaneously. Arena floods and rises in phases.
Weakness
Fire magic (evaporates water, cracks earth with heat) and Light magic (illuminates hidden stone weak points).
Fight mechanic
Arena alternates between flooded (Water phase) and solid stone maze (Earth phase) every 90s. Players must adapt strategy mid-fight.
Divine power granted
Vaelmur's Tide — tsunami clears entire floor of enemies. Stat boost: Mana +25%. Visual: water shimmer on body. World power: floods the Crimson Frontier.
DUR
Durrath the Unmoved Mountain
God of Earth & Fire — Tower of Stonecrown
EarthFire
Durrath is the world's spine — the deep rock that holds everything above it. He is ancient silence punctuated by catastrophic violence, as earthquakes and volcanic eruptions are his breathing. Earth as permanence, Fire as the molten core of the world. His physical form is a mountain given will — a colossal stone titan with rivers of lava flowing through cracks in his skin. He speaks rarely, and when he does, the ground cracks. He respects only those who stand firm against him.
Elements
Earth (primary) + Fire (secondary). Ground slams create lava fissures. Stone walls periodically erupt with fire jets.
Weakness
Water magic (quenches lava) and Dark magic (corrupts the stone — BeastFolk Dark Fang applies Rend to his stone hide).
Fight mechanic
Phase 2: Durrath sinks into the floor, reshaping the entire arena. Players must navigate a changed maze while he attacks from below through lava vents.
Divine power granted
Durrath's Mantle — raises permanent stone barriers anywhere on the map for 60s. Stat boost: Vitality +25%. Visual: floating stone plates orbit the character. World power: raises terrain in Valdris.
AET
Aethon the First Dawn
God of Light & Water — Tower of Solaran
LightWater
Aethon was the first light that ever pierced darkness — and the first rain that followed. He embodies revelation and renewal: Light as truth and clarity, Water as healing and flow. Of all the gods, Aethon is the most benevolent, but also the most merciless judge. He does not destroy for destruction's sake — he purges what he deems unworthy with blinding precision. His form is a towering figure of pure white radiance with cascading water flowing from his body like robes, his face too bright to look upon directly.
Elements
Light (primary) + Water (secondary). Blinding radiance bursts and cleansing water waves that heal enemies mid-fight.
Weakness
Dark magic (corrupts his Light core) and Earth magic (grounds water, blocks light beams with stone barriers).
Fight mechanic
Phase 3: the arena becomes blinding — all players are permanently blinded unless they cast Dark magic or step into one of three shadow pillars on the floor. Punishes pure Light builds hard.
Divine power granted
Aethon's Judgement — divine light beam strikes one target for massive damage, bypassing all defenses. Stat boost: Aura +25%. Visual: blinding halo above head. World power: forces Clear weather globally.
XAR
Xarathos the Hollow King
God of Dark & Storm — Tower of Voidspire
DarkStorm
Xarathos is the space between stars — the void that existed before any element was born. Dark as the absence of all things, Storm as the chaos that disrupts them. He is neither evil nor good; he simply unmakes. Ancient scrolls describe him as a figure of absolute shadow with crackling void-lightning coursing through his hollow form, his eyes the only visible part of him — two points of cold violet light in complete darkness. He is the only god who actively wants to be defeated, for he believes only the worthy deserve to exist.
Elements
Dark (primary) + Storm (secondary). Shadow clones multiply in darkness. Void lightning strikes ignore armor entirely.
Weakness
Light magic (reveals and locks his true form) and Fire magic (his void energy is vulnerable to pure heat — the only element he cannot absorb).
Fight mechanic
Phase 1–3 all fought in near-total darkness. His true form only visible when hit by Light magic. Players must keep a Light source active while Void Feeders attack anyone using Light.
Divine power granted
Xarathos's Void — create a 20m zone of total darkness and silence for 30s. All enemies inside are blinded and silenced. Stat boost: all stats +10%. Visual: shadow tendrils trail the character. World power: triggers a Blood Moon immediately.
VEL
Velthar the Unending Tempest
God of Storm & Light — Tower of Stormreach
StormLight
Velthar is the storm that never ends and the lightning that illuminates in a flash before the darkness returns. Storm as perpetual motion and violence, Light as the brief, blinding clarity of a lightning strike. He is the most kinetic of the gods — he never stands still, never stops moving, and his fight reflects this entirely. His form is a whirlwind of crackling energy and radiant light that shifts and changes constantly, never settling into a single shape. To face Velthar is to fight an idea as much as a being.
Elements
Storm (primary) + Light (secondary). Constant movement — teleports every 5s. Light blinds and Storm disorients simultaneously.
Weakness
Earth magic (grounds electricity, anchors his movement) and Water magic (conducts and redirects lightning back at him).
Fight mechanic
Velthar never stays still — the fight is a moving chase across a shrinking arena. Phase 3: the floor electrifies and players must chase him across floating platforms while the arena collapses beneath them.
Divine power granted
Velthar's Tempest — summon a storm that follows you for 20s, striking all nearby enemies with lightning every 2s. Stat boost: all stats +15%, attack speed +10%. Visual: lightning arcs across skin. World power: forces Thunderstorm globally.

Chapter VII
Combat System

Fast and fluid combat built around combos, parry windows, charged attacks, and layered status effects. Every mechanic feeds into every other.

Combo System
Light attacks build a combo counter (max 5, +8% damage per stack). Heavy attack consumes all stacks (×1.2 per stack). Missing or taking a hit resets counter. Dodging preserves stacks.
Stamina Bar
Dodge costs 15. Sprint costs 5/s. Running out causes a 1s exhaust stagger. Landing attacks restores Stamina. Killing an enemy fully restores Stamina instantly.
Mana Bar
All abilities cost Mana (15–60). Mana Break lockout (2s) if cast at zero. Restores 15% on kill, 10 on parry. Elf Mana Overflow converts surplus into a shield.
Parry Windows
0–0.15s: Perfect parry (full negate, 0.8s counter window ×2.0 damage). 0.15–0.35s: Good parry (80% negate, 0.4s window ×1.4). 0.35s+: Failed parry (full damage +20%). BeastFolk cannot parry.
Charged Attacks
Hold heavy attack 1.2s for ×3.0 damage. Interruptible by any hit during charge. Partial charge (0.6s) deals ×1.5. At 5 combo stacks: ×3.0 base × ×2.0 combo = ×6.0 — highest single hit possible.
Cooldowns
Every ability has its own CD. Short (8–20s): bread and butter. Long (45–120s): fight-changers. Apex abilities have longest CDs but reset on boss kills in towers.
Status Effects
Burn
4% max HP/s for 4s. Stacks ×3. Extinguished by Water or Rain. +15% damage taken while active.
Stun
1–2s full lockout. Diminishing returns — each subsequent stun in 30s is 50% shorter.
Bleed
3% HP/s for 6s. Cannot be blocked. Stacks ×2. Healed by standing still 3s.
Chill
−35% move speed, −20% attack speed for 5s. Chill + Earth = Frozen (1.5s hard stun, no DR).
Root
Cannot move for 2–4s. Can still attack. Rooted targets take +20% magic damage.
Curse
−20% magic resistance. Cannot regen Mana for duration (8s). Stacks with Rune Brand for +50% total magic amp.
Blind
No crits. Detection range to 3m. Cannot parry magic. 1.5s duration.
Rend
−15% physical armour for 8s. Stacks ×2 (30% total). Applied by Dark Fang and BeastFolk Apex Slam.
Chapter VIII
World & Weather

The world of Valdris is built around six tower regions, a neutral hub city, and a dynamic weather system that affects gameplay in real time.

Valdris — The Crossroads
The neutral spawn city where all players begin. A grand medieval city at the junction of three ancient roads. Features the Grand Market, Fighter's Guild, Duel Arena, and tower portals. Full safe zone — no PvP.
The Ashen Wastes
Permanent PvP zone. Cursed barren lands corrupted by dark magic. Permanent ash storm weather. Rare dark-magic crafting materials. No safe zones. Best loot for Tiers 1–3.
The Crimson Frontier
War-scarred borderlands. Full PvP. Weekly territory wars between races. Relic weapons and Tier 4–5 gear. The winning race gets server-wide bonuses for 7 days.
The Eternal Maw
A 10-floor bidirectional dungeon beneath Valdris. Enter at the mid-point — climb or descend. Boss-gated floors. Boss respawns every 45 minutes. Daily chest per floor. Up to 4 players.

Full Progression Path

From spawn in Valdris to True Divinity — every step of the journey.

I
Stage 1 — Mortal (Tiers 1–2)
Spawn in Valdris. Choose race. Begin evolving through Tier 1 and 2 stages. Access tower floors 1–3 (Threshold to Gauntlet). Recommended first towers: Tidehollow or Stonecrown. Earn common and uncommon gear.
II
Stage 2 — Awakened (Tiers 3–4)
Reach the In-Between evolution stage. Access tower floors 4–7. All 6 towers partially accessible. First divine fragments drop at Floor 5 demi-god boss. Skill tree fully branched by end of this stage.
III
Stage 3 — Apex (Tier 5)
Reach the Apex evolution form. Access all tower floors including Floor 10. Challenge the elemental gods. Begin earning Godly Powers. Each god defeated adds to the visual transformation stack.
IV
Stage 4 — God Rank
Conquer at least one tower completely. Divine ability slot unlocks. Prestige title displays above your name. Server-wide announcement on first God Rank. Begin working toward all 6 towers.
V
Stage 5 — True Divine
Conquer all 6 elemental towers. Apex Tower unlocks. Complete all 10 floors. Defeat the mirror boss. Achieve True Divinity — all godly powers active, full visual transformation, "True Divine" title. The pinnacle of Valdris.
